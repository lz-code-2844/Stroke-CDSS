# Outline_of_multi-agent_design

## Supervisor and agent orchestration
The multi-agent framework was organized around a supervisor agent that coordinated execution order, routing logic, and information flow across downstream agents. For each case, the supervisor first collected the available clinical, laboratory, and imaging inputs and then dispatched them to task-specific agents according to a predefined clinical workflow. The system followed a gated sequential design intended to mimic routine AIS emergency decision-making, such that later steps depended on the outputs.

At the orchestration level, the supervisor maintained a shared structured case state containing key fields generated by upstream agents, including suspected diagnosis, treatment-window variables, hemorrhage status, stroke severity summary, imaging findings, large vessel occlusion (LVO) status, contraindications, and unresolved uncertainties. When an upstream agent identified an exclusionary result or major safety signal, the case was redirected away from the reperfusion pathway or sent for additional review.

## Task-specific agents
The triage agent performed an initial, symptom-oriented, sensitivity-prioritized screening step using the chief complaint, history of present illness, symptom evolution, and focal or lateralizing neurological deficits. Cases with presentations compatible with suspected acute cerebrovascular disease, including possible AIS, were routed into the stroke pathway for downstream assessment, while presentations clearly distinguishable from stroke were routed into the non-stroke pathway.

The time-calculation agent extracted time-window information from the chief complaint and cross-checked it with detailed temporal descriptions in the history of present illness, calculating the interval from symptom onset, or last-known-well/last-seen-normal, to completion of emergency imaging or clinical evaluation.

The hemorrhage safety-routing agent served as an early safety gate based on admission non-contrast computed tomography (NCCT). It screened for intracranial hemorrhage before downstream reperfusion reasoning.

The National Institutes of Health Stroke Scale (NIHSS) agent estimated stroke severity from neurological deficits and structured documentation.

The imaging agent integrated NCCT, CTA, and CTP inputs to produce structured neuroimaging findings including hemorrhage status, early ischemic change, large vessel occlusion features, lesion laterality, vascular territory, and perfusion abnormality.

The contraindication agent screened guideline-defined contraindications and high-risk conditions.

The decision-physician agent integrated all upstream outputs to generate the final guideline-consistent acute management recommendation.

## Utility agents
Fact-extractor, consistency, and summary agents were implemented. The fact-extractor structured unstructured text. The consistency agent cross-checked concordance between clinical and imaging findings and flagged uncertainty. The summary agent generated a multidisciplinary team-style handoff summary.

## Inter-agent information flow and structured outputs
The workflow followed a sequential process: fact extraction, triage, time-window assessment, hemorrhage screening, severity estimation, imaging interpretation, contraindication screening, and final decision synthesis. Outputs were stored in a shared structured case state.

Core fields included suspected diagnosis, time variables, hemorrhage status, NIHSS severity, ASPECTS-related information, large vessel occlusion status, lesion laterality, vascular territory, perfusion abnormality, contraindication profile, and uncertainty flags. These were inputs to the decision-physician agent, which produced the final recommendation (intravenous thrombolysis, endovascular thrombectomy, standard medical treatment, or non-AIS pathway).

## IVT decision clarification
The time-calculation agent extracted onset-to-imaging intervals combined with hemorrhage, NIHSS, imaging, and contraindication outputs. The decision-physician agent generated the final IVT recommendation. All final decisions were made exclusively by the decision-physician agent.
